# Code of Conduct — `mongodb-voyageai`

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers of `mongodb-voyageai` pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

**Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:**

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experience levels
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users
- Showing empathy toward other community members

**Examples of unacceptable behavior:**

- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — including the repository, issues, pull requests, and any other communication channels used by this project — as well as public spaces where an individual is representing `mongodb-voyageai`.

## Project Context

`mongodb-voyageai` is a Rust client library for generating embeddings and reranking via [VoyageAI](https://voyageai.com). Contributions may involve:

- Core HTTP client logic (`reqwest`)
- Serialization/deserialization (`serde`)
- Error handling (`thiserror`)
- Async runtime behavior (`tokio`)
- Observability (`tracing`)
- Benchmarks (`criterion`) and integration tests (`mockito`)

When reviewing or contributing to any of these areas, please maintain a constructive and technically rigorous tone. Disagreements about design decisions should remain focused on technical merit.

## Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a private issue or contacting the maintainers directly via the repository's contact information. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated and will result in a response that is deemed necessary and appropriate to the circumstances.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by the project's leadership.
